Nikola Tesla

Inventor and engineer

A brief history of the important events in Tesla's life

  • 1856 - Nikola Tesla was born in a village Smiljan (present-day Croatia) in a Serbian family.
  • 1870 - Tesla moved north to Karlovac to attend high school at the Higher Real Gymnasium. Tesla later wrote that he became interested in demonstrations of electricity by his physics professor.
  • 1875 - He then studied electrical engineering at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz, but he never graduated from the university.
  • 1882 - Tesla moved to Paris and started working for Continental Edison. There he gained a great deal of practical experience in electrical engineering.
  • 1884 - He emigrated to the United States, and he began working almost immediately at the Thomas Edison's company Machine Works in New York. He quit after six months because of disagreements.
  • 1886 - The Tesla Electric Light & Manufacturing company was formed in New Jersey. Tesla worked for the rest of the year obtaining the patents. Check out the list of Tesla's patents.
  • 1887 - He developed an induction motor that ran on alternating current (AC), a power system format that was rapidly expanding in Europe and the United States because of its advantages in long-distance, high-voltage transmission.
  • 1888 - Tesla held a lecture about the advantages of alternating current at the American Institute of Electrical Engineers.
  • 1891 - He patented his Tesla coil, a type of resonant transformer circuit, which is widely used today in radio and television sets and other electronic equipment.
  • 1893 - Westinghouse used Tesla’s alternating current system to light the World’s Columbian Exposition at Chicago. This success was a factor in their winning the contract to install the first power machinery at Niagara Falls.
  • 1894 - In his own laboratory he experimented with shadowgraphs similar to those that later were to be used by Wilhelm Röntgen when he discovered X-rays in 1895.
  • 1895 - A fire breaks out in his laboratory. The fire not only set back Tesla's ongoing projects, it destroyed a collection of early notes and research material, models, and demonstration pieces.
  • 1897 - He patents a series of inventions that are the foundation for modern radio technology.
  • 1898 - Tesla demonstrates his remote control boat and patents his device for remote control.
  • 1899 - He moved and set up an experimental station at high altitude in Colorado Springs. At his lab, Tesla proved that the earth was a conductor, and he produced artificial lightning with discharges consisting of millions of volts, and up to 135 feet (ca. 41m) long.
  • 1900 -Tesla began construction of Wardenclyffe Tower, a wireless world broadcasting tower on Long Island. The project was abandoned because of a financial struggles. In December 1901, Marconi successfully transmitted the letter S from England to Newfoundland, defeating Tesla in the race to be first to complete such a transmission.
  • 1906 - He worked on his bladeless turbine. Because of a lack of funds, his ideas was never made into a practical device.
  • 1943 - Tesla died of heart failure at the age of 86 in the New York. All of his property inherited Tesla's nephew and everything was later moved in Nikola Tesla Museum in Belgrade.

Other interesting facts about Tesla

  • Tesla never married, but had a few close friends. Among them were the writers Robert Underwood Johnson, Mark Twain, and Francis Marion Crawford.
  • He walked to the park every day to feed the pigeons.
  • Tesla became a vegetarian in his later years, living on only milk, bread, honey, and vegetable juices.
  • For exercise, he walked between 8 and 10 miles (ca. 13 and 16 km) per day.
  • Tesla was a polyglot, speaking eight languages: Serbo-Croatian, Czech, English, French, German, Hungarian, Italian, and Latin.
  • He typically did not make drawings by hand but worked from memory.
  • On one occasion at his laboratory, Tesla worked for a period of 84 hours without rest.
  • Unit measure tesla (symbol T) was defined in 1960 in honor of the Nikola Tesla.
